Dental clinics. Property management. Recruitment agencies. Law firms. HVAC contractors.

No followers. No newsletter. No content calendar.

**Tweet 6:**

72-hour build sprints. Not 3-month development cycles.

Nobody says "I built a multi-agent orchestration pipeline."

Business owners buy results. Not architecture.

General AI assistants → competing with ChatGPT

Chatbots for everyone → most oversaturated niche in AI

The anti-portfolio framework: High hype + Low willingness to pay + Many competitors = Avoid.

"I do AI automation for anyone" = no credibility, no referrals, no compounding expertise.

"I automate patient scheduling for dental clinics" = trust, referrals, and your 5th deployment is 10x faster than your first.

**Tweet 10:**
The pattern is clear:

Low hype + High willingness to pay + Few competitors = opportunity.

The boring stuff pays because it's boring. Hype drives talent toward sexy problems, leaving the boring ones underserved.

## Thread 2: "The Niche Scoring Matrix"

**Tweet 1:**
The AI automation niche scoring matrix — how to find a $10k/mo opportunity in 48 hours.

This is the framework quiet operators use before writing a single line of code: 🧵

**Tweet 2:**
Score each niche on 6 dimensions (1-10):

• Pain Level — Would someone cry doing this task for 8 hours?
• Willingness to Pay — Does this industry already spend on solutions?
• Competition (inverted) — How many AI solutions exist? Fewer = better
• Technical Feasibility — Can you build it in 2-4 weeks?
• Market Size — 50+ potential clients?
• Your Unfair Advantage — Domain knowledge, connections, language?

**Tweet 3:**
Key: Weight Pain + Willingness to Pay at 2x.

A painful problem with willing payers beats a technically elegant solution to a mild inconvenience. Every time.

**Tweet 4:**
Once you score above threshold, validate in 48 hours:

Hours 0-8: Research sprint (Reddit, job boards, industry forums)
Hours 8-16: Find 10 businesses, cold outreach with a question — NOT a pitch
Hours 16-24: Run 3-5 discovery calls
Hours 24-48: If 3+ people confirm the pain AND would pay → validated niche

**Tweet 5:**
The 5 discovery questions:

1. "Walk me through how you handle [task]"
2. "What's the most frustrating part?"
3. "How many hours/week does it take?"
4. "Have you tried any tools?"
5. "If I cut that time by 80%, what would that be worth?"

Listen for emotional language. "I hate it" = gold.

**Tweet 6:**
The sweet spot signal:

Low hype + High willingness to pay + Few competitors

67,000 vet clinics in the US. Nobody's building AI automation for them.

300,000 property management companies. Their software looks like it was built in 2010.

That's the gap. Walk into it.
