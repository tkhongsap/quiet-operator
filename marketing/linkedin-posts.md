## Post 3: "The Framework"
*Goal: Share the Niche Scoring Matrix as free value. Authority builder.*

---

How do you find a $10k/month AI automation opportunity?

I've been studying developers who quietly make $10-50k/mo with AI services. They all have one thing in common: they picked the right niche before they wrote a line of code.

Here's the framework they use. I call it the 𝗡𝗶𝗰𝗵𝗲 𝗦𝗰𝗼𝗿𝗶𝗻𝗴 𝗠𝗮𝘁𝗿𝗶𝘅.

Score each niche candidate on 6 dimensions (1-10):

𝗣𝗮𝗶𝗻 𝗟𝗲𝘃𝗲𝗹 — How much does the manual process hurt? Look for high turnover, overtime, complaints. If employees would cry doing the task for 8 hours straight, score it high.

𝗪𝗶𝗹𝗹𝗶𝗻𝗴𝗻𝗲𝘀𝘀 𝘁𝗼 𝗣𝗮𝘆 — Does this industry already spend money on solutions? Check their existing software budgets.

𝗖𝗼𝗺𝗽𝗲𝘁𝗶𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻 (𝗶𝗻𝘃𝗲𝗿𝘁𝗲𝗱) — How many AI-specific solutions exist? Fewer = better.

𝗧𝗲𝗰𝗵𝗻𝗶𝗰𝗮𝗹 𝗙𝗲𝗮𝘀𝗶𝗯𝗶𝗹𝗶𝘁𝘆 — Can you build a working solution in 2-4 weeks?

𝗠𝗮𝗿𝗸𝗲𝘁 𝗦𝗶𝘇𝗲 — Enough potential clients for 50+ customers?

𝗬𝗼𝘂𝗿 𝗨𝗻𝗳𝗮𝗶𝗿 𝗔𝗱𝘃𝗮𝗻𝘁𝗮𝗴𝗲 — Domain knowledge, connections, language, geography?

Key: Weight Pain + Willingness to Pay at 2x. A painful problem with willing payers beats everything else.

The sweet spot signal: 𝗟𝗼𝘄 𝗵𝘆𝗽𝗲 + 𝗛𝗶𝗴𝗵 𝘄𝗶𝗹𝗹𝗶𝗻𝗴𝗻𝗲𝘀𝘀 𝘁𝗼 𝗽𝗮𝘆 + 𝗙𝗲𝘄 𝗰𝗼𝗺𝗽𝗲𝘁𝗶𝘁𝗼𝗿𝘀.

Nobody's posting Twitter threads about veterinary clinic automation. There are 67,000 vet clinics in the US alone. That's your opening.

The boring stuff pays because it's boring. The hype drives talent toward sexy problems, leaving the boring problems underserved.

Walk straight into the gap.
