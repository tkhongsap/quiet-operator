# Client Pitch Deck — AI Automation Services

> **8-slide deck for pitching to non-tech business owners.** Each slide has a title, key message, talking points, and visual guidance. Designed for someone who's never pitched before.

---

## Before You Present

- **Keep it under 15 minutes.** Non-tech buyers lose interest fast. Aim for 10.
- **Use their language.** Say "save your team 15 hours a week," not "LLM-powered workflow orchestration."
- **Bring a live demo.** Slides set the stage. The demo closes the deal.
- **Practice the transitions.** The flow between slides matters more than individual slides.

---

## Slide 1: The Hook

**Title:** "What If [Specific Pain Point] Just... Stopped?"

**Key Message:** Name their exact pain. Make it visceral.

**Talking Points:**
- "Your team spends [X hours/week] on [manual task]. That's [Y hours/year]."
- "Every hour on [task] is an hour NOT spent on [higher-value activity]."
- "What would change if that time came back?"

**Visual:** A single, large number — the hours wasted per year. Nothing else. Let it land.

---

## Slide 2: The Problem (Their World Today)

**Title:** "Here's What [Task] Looks Like Right Now"

**Key Message:** Walk through their current manual workflow step by step.

**Talking Points:**
- Step 1: [Email comes in / form is submitted / call is received]
- Step 2: [Someone manually copies data to a spreadsheet]
- Step 3: [Someone else reviews and categorizes]
- Step 4: [Someone sends a follow-up / enters into system]
- "Each step is a chance for errors, delays, and things falling through cracks."

**Visual:** A simple flowchart of their current process with red "pain points" marked (delays, errors, manual steps).

---

## Slide 3: The Cost of Doing Nothing

**Title:** "What This Costs You Every Month"

**Key Message:** Translate their pain into dollars (USD).

**Talking Points:**
- "[X hours/week] × [$Y/hour labor cost] = $[Z]/month in manual labor"
- "Plus hidden costs: errors that need fixing, delays that lose customers, employee burnout"
- "Over 12 months, that's $[annual total] spent on a problem that can be solved"

**Visual:** A bar chart or simple table: Monthly cost, Annual cost, 3-year cost. Make the numbers undeniable.

---

## Slide 4: The Solution (Their World After)

**Title:** "Here's How It Works With Automation"

**Key Message:** Show the same workflow, but automated. Contrast with Slide 2.

**Talking Points:**
- "Same trigger — [email / form / call] — but now it's handled automatically"
- "AI reads, categorizes, and routes in seconds instead of hours"
- "Your team only steps in for exceptions — the 5% that need human judgment"
- "Everything else runs in the background, 24/7, without errors"

**Visual:** The same flowchart from Slide 2, but simplified — most manual steps replaced with a single "AI Automation" block. Green checkmarks where red pain points were.

---

## Slide 5: Live Demo (The Money Slide)

**Title:** "Let Me Show You"

**Key Message:** Run the automation live with real (or realistic) data.

**Talking Points:**
- "I'm going to run a real [invoice / lead / appointment] through the system right now"
- [Run the demo — show data flowing, output appearing]
- "That just took [X seconds]. Your team currently takes [Y minutes]."
- "And it works the same way at 10 PM on a Friday."

**Visual:** Your screen. Live demo. No slides needed — this IS the visual. Share your screen and let them watch it work.

---

## Slide 6: Results From Similar Businesses

**Title:** "Businesses Like Yours Are Already Using This"

**Key Message:** Social proof. Show that this works for businesses in their industry.

**Talking Points:**
- "[Industry] business reduced [metric] by [percentage]"
- "A [similar-sized company] saves [X hours/week] and [$Y/month]"
- "[Quote from a similar client, if available]"
- "These are real results, not projections"

**Visual:** 2-3 short case study cards: Company type, Problem, Result, with specific numbers. Anonymized is fine — "A 10-person dental practice in Bangkok" is credible.

---

## Slide 7: Your Investment

**Title:** "What It Costs vs. What It Saves"

**Key Message:** Frame the price as ROI, not cost.

**Talking Points:**
- "Setup: $[X] (one-time) — covers building and configuring your automation"
- "Monthly: $[Y]/month — includes monitoring, maintenance, and support"
- "Your current cost for this process: $[Z]/month"
- "Net savings: $[Z - Y]/month. ROI from month one."
- "30-day money-back guarantee — if the automation doesn't deliver, you pay nothing"

**Visual:** A simple 2-column comparison: "Without Automation" vs. "With Automation." Monthly cost, annual cost, ROI. Make it a no-brainer.

> **All prices in USD.** Adjust for local currency when presenting to regional clients.

---

## Slide 8: Next Steps

**Title:** "Here's How We Get Started"

**Key Message:** Make it easy to say yes. Remove friction.

**Talking Points:**
- "Step 1: 30-minute kickoff call — we map your exact workflow (this week)"
- "Step 2: I build and test the automation (1-2 weeks)"
- "Step 3: We go live — you start saving time immediately"
- "No disruption to your current operations during setup"
- "Questions?"

**Visual:** A simple 3-step timeline graphic. Clean, minimal, with checkmarks. End with your contact info and a clear call-to-action.

---

## Deck Design Tips

- **Background:** Clean white or very light gray. Not dark mode — non-tech buyers expect traditional presentation style.
- **Font:** One sans-serif font (Inter, Helvetica, or similar). No fancy fonts.
- **Colors:** Your brand blue + green for positive metrics + red for pain points. Max 3 colors.
- **Text per slide:** Maximum 20 words. If you need more words, you need more slides.
- **No jargon:** Zero mentions of AI models, APIs, LLMs, agents, or technical architecture. Ever.
- **Tools:** Google Slides (free, shareable), Canva (templates), or Keynote. Don't overthink this.
