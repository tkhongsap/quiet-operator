# 20 Pre-Researched Niche Ideas for AI Automation

**Scoring Dimensions (each 1-10):**
- **Pain:** How painful is the current manual process?
- **Pay:** Willingness and ability to pay for a solution
- **Comp:** Competition level (10 = low competition, 1 = saturated)
- **Tech:** Technical feasibility with current AI tools
- **Market:** Market size (number of potential clients)

**Score = (Pain × 2) + (Pay × 2) + Comp + Tech + Market** (Pain and Pay weighted 2x)

## Tier 1: Highest Potential (Score 45+)

### 1. Dental/Medical Clinic Appointment & Follow-Up Automation
**Pain: 8 | Pay: 8 | Comp: 7 | Tech: 9 | Market: 8 | Score: 56**

Automate appointment reminders, no-show follow-ups, post-visit feedback collection, and insurance pre-authorization checks. Clinics currently use front desk staff for these tasks, leading to missed appointments (average no-show rate ~20%) and lost revenue. AI agent monitors the schedule, sends personalized reminders via SMS/LINE, follows up with no-shows, and collects reviews from satisfied patients. Setup: $3,000. Retainer: $800-1,500/month.

### 2. Recruitment Agency Resume Screening & Candidate Outreach
**Pain: 9 | Pay: 8 | Comp: 6 | Tech: 8 | Market: 7 | Score: 53**

Recruitment agencies manually screen hundreds of resumes per role. AI agent ingests job descriptions, scores incoming resumes against requirements, generates shortlists with reasoning, and drafts personalized outreach emails in the candidate's language (critical for Thai/English bilingual markets). Handles the "80% obvious rejects" automatically so recruiters focus on the top 20%. Setup: $5,000. Retainer: $1,500-3,000/month.

### 3. Property Management Tenant Communication & Maintenance Coordination
**Pain: 8 | Pay: 7 | Comp: 8 | Tech: 9 | Market: 8 | Score: 55**

Property managers handle repetitive tenant inquiries (lease terms, payment dates, maintenance requests) across dozens or hundreds of units. AI agent responds to tenant messages (email/LINE/WhatsApp), creates and routes maintenance tickets, sends rent reminders, and generates monthly owner reports. The pain is extreme — property managers are essentially glorified message routers. Setup: $3,000. Retainer: $1,000-2,000/month per management company.

### 4. E-commerce Seller Customer Inquiry Response (Shopee/Lazada)
**Pain: 9 | Pay: 6 | Comp: 6 | Tech: 8 | Market: 9 | Score: 53**

Thai e-commerce sellers on Shopee/Lazada answer the same 20 questions hundreds of times: "Is this in stock?", "When will it ship?", "Can I get a discount?" AI agent monitors seller chat, auto-responds to routine inquiries in Thai, escalates complex issues to the human seller, and tracks response time metrics. 300,000+ active Thai e-commerce sellers, most handling customer service manually. Setup: $1,500. Retainer: $300-800/month (high volume, lower price point).

### 5. Law Firm Document Intake & Case Summarization
**Pain: 9 | Pay: 9 | Comp: 7 | Tech: 7 | Market: 6 | Score: 54**

Law firms receive mountains of documents for each case — contracts, correspondence, court filings, evidence. AI agent ingests new documents, extracts key facts, generates case summaries, identifies relevant precedents, and flags deadlines. Lawyers currently spend 30-40% of billable time on document review. Even a 50% reduction is worth thousands per month. Setup: $5,000-10,000. Retainer: $2,000-5,000/month.

## How to Use This List

1. **Start with your unfair advantage:** If you know an industry, start there — domain knowledge is worth +3 points on any score
2. **Validate before building:** These scores are based on research, not validation. Run the 48-hour validation sprint from Module 2 before committing
3. **Geographic adaptation:** Scores may shift based on your market. E-commerce seller support scores higher in Thailand/SEA; law firm automation scores higher in US/UK
4. **Don't pick the "best" niche — pick YOUR best niche:** A score of 49 in an industry where you have connections beats a score of 56 in an industry where you know nobody
5. **Combine niches cautiously:** "Dental + medical clinics" is fine (similar workflows). "Dental + construction" is not (totally different problems, clients, and language)
